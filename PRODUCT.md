# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Resellers who buy bulk secondhand-clothing bundles through Fleek — 10 to 50+ unsorted garments arriving as a single box, no individual listings, no photos, no grading. They open this app immediately after unpacking, standing over the pile with their phone in hand, working through it item by item. The job is turning that physical pile into individual, ready-to-post marketplace listings (title, condition, defects, price) as fast as possible, without the tedium of photographing, describing, and pricing each piece by hand.

## Product Purpose

A photo-capture-to-listing pipeline: the reseller rapid-captures a few photos of one item, an AI pipeline returns a structured listing (title, category, condition grade, defects, description, suggested price), and it lands in a dashboard they can sort, edit, and mark as posted. It exists because Fleek's own tooling ends at delivery — the reseller is on their own for everything downstream, and that per-item cataloging step is the most tedious part of running a resale business. Success looks like a full bundle turned into a sorted, price-ready listing dashboard in the time it used to take to photograph one item properly.

## Positioning

Turn a box of unsorted secondhand clothes into ready-to-post listings in minutes.

## Brand Personality

Calm, considered, premium. The interface should feel like handling a well-made physical object, not operating a busy dashboard — unhurried, trustworthy, quietly confident. It should not read as a typical resale/marketplace app: no busy colorful listing grids, no gamified badges, no cluttered card walls. Confidence is shown through restraint and precision, not through decoration.

## Anti-references

No neon accents or saturated primary colors. No flat, sticker-style icon UI. No generic SaaS-white dashboard look (stark white backgrounds, harsh black text, boxy card grids). No typical marketplace/resale-app aesthetic — avoid anything that reads like Depop, Vinted, or eBay's dense, colorful, listing-grid style. The interface should feel closer to a premium physical-goods app (fashion resale, appraisal, concierge service) than a utility tool.

## Design Principles

- **Restraint over decoration.** Every visual choice should feel deliberate and quiet; when in doubt, remove rather than add.
- **The interface disappears into the task.** The reseller is standing over a pile of clothes working fast — chrome, ornamentation, and unnecessary steps are friction against that.
- **Material, not flat.** Favor natural, tactile-feeling neutrals and subtle depth over flat corporate color blocking — the product should feel like it has texture even though it's a screen.
- **Motion earns its place.** Small, purposeful animation (a card settling into the dashboard, a status flipping) reinforces what happened; it never exists just to look modern.
- **One confident accent, not a palette of them.** A single considered accent color carries emphasis (price, primary actions); everything else stays neutral so that accent means something when it appears.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast throughout. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` with a crossfade/instant-transition fallback for every animation. Never signal meaning by color alone — condition grades (A–D) always carry the letter, not just a color dot; status (draft/posted) always carries a label, not just a badge color.
